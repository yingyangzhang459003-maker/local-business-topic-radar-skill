# Source Strategy

Research each candidate in the following order. Record direct URLs and publication dates for every source used in a Topic Card.

## 1. Local official information

Start with city, district, government, public-service, school, venue, transport, and regulator notices. Use these sources for policy, schedules, eligibility, safety, public services, and other material local facts.

## 2. Local media and life signals

Check reputable local news, community-service reporting, event calendars, and observable life signals. Treat these as discovery leads and corroborate claims that could change a customer's decision.

## 3. Platform discussions

Review public local discussions and search behavior for repeated questions, decision tension, and language customers use. Platform posts are signals, not proof; do not present anecdotes as verified facts.

## 4. National industry signals with a provable local bridge

Use national policy, industry, seasonal, or consumer signals only when a source proves a specific city or district connection. Without that bridge, do not call the topic local.

## Research tools and fallback

Use Agent Reach when it is installed to discover and document sources. When it is unavailable, use the current runtime web search as the fallback. In either path, retain source URLs, dates, scope, and uncertainty; never replace unavailable evidence with invented local facts.
